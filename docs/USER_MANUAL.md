## 1. Introduction and System Overview

### 1.1 What is Vibely POS?

Vibely POS is a modern point-of-sale system designed for small to medium businesses. It helps you manage sales, inventory, customers, and daily operations all in one place. Whether you run a retail store, a cafe, or a small shop, Vibely POS makes it easy to process transactions, track stock, and understand your business performance.

[Screenshot: Vibely POS login screen with the app logo and welcome message]

### 1.2 Key Features

Vibely POS provides a complete set of tools to run your business:

- **Authentication and Security**: Secure login with role-based access control. Different team members have different permission levels.
- **Dashboard**: A real-time overview of your business performance, including today's sales, transaction count, low stock alerts, and shift status.
- **Point of Sale (Checkout)**: A fast and intuitive checkout process that supports both cash and card payments.
- **Inventory Management**: Track all your products, monitor stock levels, and receive alerts when items are running low.
- **Categories**: Organize your products into categories for easier management and reporting.
- **Sales History**: View all past transactions, search for specific sales, and process refunds when needed.
- **Customer Management**: Keep a database of your customers, track their purchase history, and manage a loyalty points program.
- **Supplier Management**: Maintain information about your vendors and suppliers.
- **Purchase Orders**: Create and manage orders to restock your inventory from suppliers.
- **Cash Shifts**: Track opening and closing balances, monitor cash flow, and identify any discrepancies.
- **Reports and Analytics**: Generate detailed reports on sales performance, trends, top products, and top customers.
- **Settings**: Configure your store information, receipt formatting, tax rates, currency, and user preferences.
- **User Management**: Add and manage team members with different roles and permissions.

### 1.3 Supported Platforms

Vibely POS works on multiple platforms:

- **Desktop**: The primary platform, optimized for Windows, macOS, and Linux computers. This is the recommended platform for most operations.
- **Android**: Works on Android devices (Android 7.0 and above).
- **iOS**: Works on iPhones and iPads (iOS 14 and above).
- **Web**: Accessible through modern web browsers (coming soon).

> **Tip:** For the best experience, use the desktop application at your main checkout counter. Use mobile devices for inventory checks or when moving around the store.

## 2. Getting Started

### 2.1 First Login

When you first open Vibely POS, you will see a login screen. Follow these steps to sign in:

1. Enter your email address in the email field.
2. Enter your password in the password field.
3. If you want the system to remember your login for future sessions, check the "Remember me" box.
4. Click the "Sign In" button.

[Screenshot: Login screen with email and password fields, remember me checkbox, and sign in button]

> **Tip:** If you are an administrator setting up the system for the first time, make sure your team members change their passwords after their first login.

### 2.2 Understanding the Navigation Menu

After logging in, you will see the main navigation menu on the left side of the screen. This menu gives you access to all the features of Vibely POS:

- **Dashboard**: The home screen with business overview
- **POS / Checkout**: Process new sales
- **Inventory**: Manage your products
- **Categories**: Organize products into categories
- **Sales History**: View past transactions
- **Customers**: Manage customer information
- **Suppliers**: Manage vendor information
- **Purchase Orders**: Create restock orders
- **Shifts**: Manage cash drawer shifts
- **Reports**: View analytics and reports
- **Settings**: Configure system preferences
- **Users**: Manage team member accounts

[Screenshot: Navigation menu with all menu items visible]

### 2.3 Common Navigation Actions

**Opening a Screen**

Click on any item in the navigation menu to open that screen. The currently active screen is highlighted in the menu.

**Returning to Dashboard**

Click on the "Dashboard" item at the top of the navigation menu or the Vibely logo to return to the home screen at any time.

**Logging Out**

To log out of the system, click on your username or profile icon (usually in the top right corner) and select "Logout" from the dropdown menu.

> **Tip:** Always log out when leaving your workstation, especially in a retail environment where multiple people use the same device.

### 2.4 Keyboard Shortcuts (Desktop)

The following keyboard shortcuts can help you work more efficiently on the desktop platform:

| Shortcut | Action |
|----------|--------|
| Ctrl + 1 | Go to Dashboard |
| Ctrl + 2 | Go to Checkout |
| Ctrl + 3 | Go to Inventory |
| Ctrl + 4 | Go to Reports |
| Ctrl + S | Save (when in a form) |
| Escape | Close dialogs or cancel actions |

## 3. Dashboard

### 3.1 Understanding the Dashboard

The Dashboard is your business command center. It provides a quick overview of how your store is performing right now. When you log in, you will see this screen first.

[Screenshot: Dashboard showing key metrics cards and recent transactions]

### 3.2 Key Metrics Cards

The top of the Dashboard displays four important metrics:

**Today's Sales**

This shows the total revenue from all sales made today. It includes both cash and card payments.

**Transactions**

This displays the number of transactions completed today. A transaction is each time a customer completes a purchase.

**Low Stock**

This indicator shows how many products have stock levels below the minimum threshold. When this number is not zero, you should check your inventory and consider placing purchase orders.

**Shift Status**

This shows whether there is currently an open shift. If it says "Open" in green, a cashier is currently working with the cash drawer. If it says "Closed" in gray, the cash drawer is not in use.

> **Tip:** Check the Dashboard first thing in the morning to see if there are any low stock alerts that need attention.

### 3.3 Recent Transactions

Below the metrics cards, you will see a list of the most recent transactions. Each row shows:

- Invoice number: A unique identifier for the sale
- Date and time: When the sale was made
- Total: The amount of the sale
- Payment method: Whether paid by cash or card

Click on any transaction to see more details.

[Screenshot: Recent transactions table with invoice numbers, dates, totals, and payment methods]

### 3.4 Quick Actions

The Dashboard includes quick action buttons that let you jump directly to common tasks:

- **New Sale**: Opens the Checkout screen to start a new transaction
- **Inventory**: Opens the Inventory screen to manage products
- **Reports**: Opens the Reports screen to view analytics

### 3.5 Refreshing Data

The Dashboard automatically updates periodically. You can also click the "Refresh" button in the top right corner to fetch the latest data immediately.

## 4. Point of Sale and Checkout

### 4.1 Accessing the Checkout Screen

To process a new sale, click on "POS / Checkout" in the navigation menu or use the "New Sale" quick action from the Dashboard.

[Screenshot: Main checkout screen split between product search and cart]

### 4.2 Searching for Products

The left side of the checkout screen shows your product catalog. Use the search bar at the top to find products:

1. Click on the search field or start typing the product name.
2. Type at least 2 characters to see search results.
3. Products matching your search will appear in a grid.
4. Click on a product to add it to the cart.

[Screenshot: Product search bar with search results displayed in a grid]

> **Tip:** Organize your products into categories and ensure product names are clear and searchable. This makes the checkout process much faster.

### 4.3 Understanding the Cart

The right side of the screen shows the current cart with all items to be purchased:

- Each item shows the product name, unit price, quantity, and subtotal.
- Use the plus (+) and minus (-) buttons to change quantities.
- Click the X button to remove an item from the cart.
- The cart shows the total number of items and the total amount due.

[Screenshot: Cart sidebar showing items, quantities, and total]

### 4.4 Processing a Sale

When you have added all items to the cart, follow these steps to complete the sale:

1. Verify the items and quantities in the cart are correct.
2. Click the "Checkout" button.
3. A payment dialog will appear showing the total amount.
4. Select the payment method: "Cash" or "Card".
5. For cash payments, enter the amount received from the customer.
6. Click "Complete Sale" to finish the transaction.
7. The system will process the payment and show a success message.

[Screenshot: Payment dialog with total amount and payment method options]

### 4.5 Cash Payments

When a customer pays with cash:

1. Select "Cash" as the payment method.
2. Enter the amount the customer gave you.
3. The system will automatically calculate the change due.
4. Complete the sale and give the customer their change along with the receipt.

### 4.6 Card Payments

When a customer pays with a card:

1. Select "Card" as the payment method.
2. The system will process the transaction through your payment terminal.
3. Wait for the payment to be approved.
4. Once approved, the sale is complete.

> **Tip:** Always confirm the payment method with the customer before processing.

### 4.7 Clearing the Cart

If you need to start over or the customer changes their mind:

1. Look for the "Clear Cart" button in the cart section.
2. Click it to remove all items from the cart.
3. The cart will be empty and ready for a new customer.

### 4.8 Handling Errors

If you encounter an error during checkout:

- A message will appear at the bottom of the screen explaining the issue.
- Common errors include product not found or payment processing failure.
- Note the error message and try again, or contact your manager for assistance.

## 5. Inventory Management

### 5.1 Understanding the Inventory Screen

The Inventory screen shows all your products in a table format. This is where you add new products, update existing ones, and monitor stock levels.

[Screenshot: Inventory screen with product table, search bar, and KPI cards]

### 5.2 Viewing Products

The main part of the screen displays a table with the following columns:

- **SKU**: The product's unique identification code
- **Name**: The product name
- **Category**: The category the product belongs to
- **Price**: The selling price
- **Stock**: Current quantity in stock
- **Size/Unit**: The unit of measurement
- **Status**: Whether stock is Good, Medium, or Low
- **Actions**: Edit or delete buttons

### 5.3 KPI Cards

Above the product table, you will see summary cards:

- **Total Products**: The total number of unique products in your inventory
- **Low Stock**: How many products are running low and need to be reordered
- **Total Value**: The total worth of your inventory (stock multiplied by price)
- **Categories**: How many categories you have created

### 5.4 Searching for Products

Use the search bar at the top to find specific products:

1. Click on the search field.
2. Type the product name or SKU.
3. The table will filter to show matching products in real-time.

### 5.5 Adding a New Product

To add a new product to your inventory:

1. Click the "Add Product" button in the top right corner.
2. A form dialog will appear.
3. Fill in the product details:
   - **Name**: Enter the product name (required)
   - **SKU**: Enter a unique code for the product
   - **Category**: Select a category from the dropdown
   - **Selling Price**: Enter the price customers will pay
   - **Cost Price**: Enter what you pay to buy the product
   - **Initial Stock**: Enter how many units you have
   - **Low Stock Threshold**: Enter the minimum quantity before alerts
   - **Unit**: Enter the unit of measurement (e.g., piece, kg, liter)
4. Click "Save" to add the product.

[Screenshot: Add product dialog with all fields]

> **Tip:** Take time to set accurate cost prices. This information is needed for profit calculations in reports.

### 5.6 Editing a Product

To update an existing product:

1. Find the product in the table.
2. Click the "Edit" (pencil) icon in the Actions column.
3. The product form will open with current values.
4. Make your changes.
5. Click "Save" to update the product.

### 5.7 Deleting a Product

To remove a product from your inventory:

1. Find the product in the table.
2. Click the "Delete" (trash) icon in the Actions column.
3. A confirmation dialog will appear.
4. Click "Delete" to confirm. This action cannot be undone.

> **Warning:** Deleting a product will also remove it from any historical sales records. Consider setting a product as inactive instead if you want to stop selling it while keeping historical data.

### 5.8 Understanding Stock Status

Products have one of three status indicators:

- **Good** (green): Stock is above the low stock threshold
- **Medium** (yellow): Stock is getting low but not critical
- **Low** (red): Stock is at or below the threshold and needs ordering

### 5.9 Pagination

If you have many products, they will be displayed across multiple pages:

- Use "Previous" and "Next" buttons at the bottom to navigate.
- You can also jump to specific pages if needed.

Categories help you organize your products into logical groups. For example, a grocery store might have categories like "Beverages," "Snacks," "Dairy," and "Produce." Using categories makes it easier to find products, run reports, and understand sales patterns.

[Screenshot: Categories screen showing top categories and full list]

### 6.2 Viewing Categories

The Categories screen shows:

- **Top Categories**: A visual grid of your most used categories
- **All Categories**: A table listing every category with details

### 6.3 KPI Cards

- **Total Categories**: Number of categories you have created
- **Total Products**: Products across all categories
- **Average per Category**: How many products you have in each category on average
- **Largest Category**: Your category with the most products

### 6.4 Adding a New Category

1. Click "Add Category" in the top right.
2. Enter a name for the category.
3. Add a description (optional).
4. Choose a color for visual identification.
5. Click "Save."

### 6.5 Editing a Category

1. Click the edit icon on the category card or row.
2. Update the name, description, or color.
3. Click "Save."

When you delete a category, products in that category become "uncategorized." They are not deleted, just no longer assigned to any category.

## 10. Purchase Orders

### 10.1 Understanding Purchase Orders

A purchase order (PO) is a document you send to a supplier to order products. It specifies what items you want, quantities, prices, and expected delivery date.

[Screenshot: Purchase orders screen with order list and status]

### 10.2 Purchase Order Statuses

Orders can have different statuses:

- **Draft**: Being prepared, not yet sent
- **Pending**: Sent to supplier, awaiting response
- **Approved**: Supplier accepted the order
- **Received**: Products arrived and were added to inventory
- **Cancelled**: Order was cancelled

### 10.3 KPI Cards

- **Total Orders**: All purchase orders in the system
- **Pending Orders**: Orders awaiting delivery
- **Total Amount**: Total value of all orders

### 10.4 Creating a Purchase Order

1. Click "Create PO" in the top right.
2. Select a supplier from the dropdown.
3. Add line items:
   - Select a product
   - Enter quantity
   - Enter unit cost (what you pay per unit)
4. Add any notes if needed.
5. Set expected delivery date (optional).
6. Click "Save" to create as draft or "Submit" to send to supplier.

[Screenshot: Create purchase order form]

> **Tip:** Review your low stock items in Inventory before creating purchase orders to ensure you order what you need.

### 10.5 Editing a Purchase Order

Only draft orders can be edited. Click the edit icon to make changes.

### 10.6 Receiving Stock

When a shipment arrives:

1. Find the purchase order.
2. Click the "Receive" button or mark as "Received."
3. The system will add the products to your inventory automatically.

### 10.7 Viewing Order Details

Click the view (eye) icon to see the full details of any purchase order.

## 11. Cash Shifts

### 11.1 Understanding Shifts

A cash shift is a period of time during which a cashier uses a cash drawer. Shifts help track money coming in and out, identify discrepancies, and maintain accountability.

[Screenshot: Shifts screen with current shift status and history]

### 11.2 Opening a Shift

At the start of a work day or shift:

1. Click "Open Shift" in the top right.
2. Enter the opening balance (amount of cash in the drawer).
3. Click "Open Shift" to start.

[Screenshot: Open shift dialog with opening balance field]

### 11.3 During the Shift

While a shift is open, the system tracks:

- Cash sales: Total paid by cash
- Card sales: Total paid by card
- Total sales: Combined total

### 11.4 Closing a Shift

At the end of the shift:

1. Click "Close Shift."
2. Enter the closing balance (actual cash in drawer).
3. The system calculates the discrepancy:
   - Positive: More cash than expected (overage)
   - Negative: Less cash than expected (shortage)
   - Zero: Perfect match
4. Click "Close Shift" to complete.

[Screenshot: Close shift dialog showing expected vs actual and discrepancy]

### 11.5 Shift Status

- **Open**: Currently active, cashier is working
- **Closed**: Shift ended, balance reconciled

### 11.6 KPI Cards

- **Open Shifts**: Number of currently active shifts
- **Today's Sales**: Total sales for today across all shifts
- **Total Discrepancy**: Sum of all overages and shortages

> **Tip:** Always count the cash drawer carefully before opening and closing shifts. Document any discrepancies and report them to management.

## 15. Troubleshooting

### 15.1 Common Login Issues

**Cannot Log In**

- Check your internet connection.
- Verify your email and password are correct.
- Contact your administrator if you forgot your password.
- Make sure your account is active (not suspended).

**Account is Suspended**

Contact your administrator to reactivate your account.

### 15.2 Checkout Problems

**Product Not Found**

- The product may not exist in the system. Add it through Inventory first.
- Check the spelling of the product name.
- Verify the product has an active status.

**Payment Processing Failed**

- For card payments, check your payment terminal connection.
- For cash payments, ensure the amounts are entered correctly.
- Try processing the sale again.
- If problems persist, contact technical support.

**Cart Won't Clear**

- Try clicking the clear button again.
- Refresh the page and start a new transaction.

### 15.3 Inventory Issues

**Cannot Add Product**

- Ensure all required fields are filled.
- Check that the SKU is unique (not already used).
- Verify price and stock values are valid numbers.

**Stock Quantity is Wrong**

- A sale may have updated the stock automatically.
- A purchase order may have been received.
- Manually adjust if needed through the edit function.

### 15.4 Report Generation Errors

**No Data Showing**

- Check that the date range is correct.
- Verify you have made sales in the selected period.
- Try refreshing the report.

**Report is Slow to Load**

- Large date ranges take longer to process.
- Try selecting a smaller time period.
- Be patient during peak reporting times.

### 15.5 Shift Problems

**Cannot Open Shift**

- Check that you are logged in.
- Verify the opening balance is entered.
- A shift may already be open (only one open shift at a time).

**Discrepancy at Close**

- Carefully count the cash in the drawer.
- Review all transactions during the shift.
- Document the discrepancy and report to management.

## 16. Frequently Asked Questions

### 16.1 General Questions

**What is Vibely POS?**

Vibely POS is a point-of-sale system that helps businesses manage sales, inventory, customers, and reporting. It is designed for small to medium retail businesses.

**What do I need to use Vibely POS?**

You need a device (computer, tablet, or phone) with an internet connection. The desktop application is recommended for the best experience.

**How much does Vibely POS cost?**

Pricing information is available from your Vibely POS provider or sales representative.

### 16.2 Account and Access

**How do I reset my password?**

Contact your administrator to reset your password. They can create a new one for you.

**Can I have multiple accounts?**

Yes, multiple team members can have their own accounts with different access levels.

**What happens if I forget to log out?**

The system has an auto-logout feature based on your settings. For security, always log out when leaving your workstation.

### 16.3 Sales and Checkout

**How do I process a refund?**

Find the original sale in Sales History, click the refund button, and confirm. The refund will be recorded and stock adjusted.

**Can I sell without internet?**

Currently, an internet connection is required. Offline mode is planned for future updates.

**What payment methods are supported?**

Vibely POS supports cash and card payments. The card integration depends on your payment processor setup.

### 16.4 Inventory

**How does stock tracking work?**

When you make a sale, the system automatically reduces the stock quantity. When you receive a purchase order, stock is increased.

**What is low stock threshold?**

This is the minimum quantity you specify for each product. When stock falls to or below this number, the product is marked as low stock.

**Can I import products in bulk?**

Currently, products must be added individually. Bulk import features are planned.

### 16.5 Reports

**How far back does report data go?**

Report data goes back to when you started using Vibely POS. All historical data is preserved.

**Can I export reports?**

Currently, reports are viewed on screen. Export features are planned for future releases.

### 16.6 Technical

**What browsers work with Vibely POS?**

For web access, use modern browsers like Chrome, Firefox, Safari, or Edge. The desktop application works on Windows, Mac, and Linux.

**How do I contact support?**

Email your support requests to the address provided by your Vibely POS administrator or check your welcome documentation.
